The most popular breeds

The following is the list of breeds popular in some countries around the World

Pug (China)

The Pug is a royal breed of dogs in China with a rich and long history in this country. These dogs were even guarded sometimes, but Empererors treasurered these dogs. With time, the breed became popular in royal houses across the world.

French Bulldog (France)

The most interesting fact about the breed is that it has roots in England, rather than France. This breed arrived to France with British immigrants who took their bulldogs when leaving their country. The French Bulldog quickly became popular and loved in France and other parts of Europe. Nowadays, the breed is widely spread acroos the United States.

Labrador (United States of America)

Labrador Retriever, or simply Labrador, came to the United States from Great Britain in the early 1900's. Labradors are widely used as disability assistance for blind people, or those with autism.

Caucasian Shepherd Dog (Russia)

The Caucasian Shepherd Dog, also known in Russia as Caucasian Ovcharka or Caucasian Mastiff, is an ancient breed from the Caucasus Mountains. In the 1900’s the USSR took interest in turning this breed into a show dog, which helped popularize the breed in Russia. Caucasian Ovcharka is a very large and strong dog. The minimum weights for females and males are 99 and 110 lbs respectively. Some male species may have a weight of 150-160 lbs. These dogs are great in colder weather, and make a great family companion.

German Shepherd (Germany)

German Shepherd is a breed of medium to large-sized working dog that originated in Germany. This is the pretty young breed, with thier origin dating 1899. Before that, during the 1800’s, Germany was facing a breed standardization problem. While shepherd dogs were popular within Germany before this time, there were no breed specifications or standards. During this time, many different areas of Germany were selectively breeding shepherds to how they believed a dog should be.

English Bulldog (England)

English, or British, Bulldog is a medium-sized breed of dog. It is a muscular, hefty dog with a wrinkled face and a distinctive pushed-in nose. Bulldogs gained popularity in the early 1800’s by bull baiting, although due to the cruel nature of this ‘sport’, it was outlawed in 1835. After the law was passed, the breed popularity was cut down until they were used once again to help seize and capture bulls. As time passed, the Bulldog quickly became a sort of ‘icon’ in British culture. As the BBC wrote in 2016: "to many the Bulldog is a national icon, symbolising pluck and determination."
